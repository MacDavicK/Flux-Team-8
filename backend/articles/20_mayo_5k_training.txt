Want to run a 5-kilometre race (about 3.1 miles) but not sure where to start? This 7-week training schedule from Mayo Clinic can help you get there, even if you've never run before.

Before You Start

Check with your healthcare provider if you've been inactive or have any health conditions. Make sure you have supportive running shoes — the right shoes can make a significant difference in comfort and injury prevention.

The goal of this training schedule is to gradually build your ability to run for 30 minutes (or complete approximately 5 km) by the end of 7 weeks. It uses a walk-run approach that allows you to build fitness gradually without overloading your body.

Key Principles of the Programme

Walk-run intervals: Alternating walking and running is the safest and most effective way for beginners to build running fitness. Walking intervals are active recovery that allow your heart rate to moderate while keeping you moving.

Three runs per week: The programme includes 3 running days per week. Rest days between sessions are just as important as the running days — muscles, tendons, and bones need recovery time to strengthen and adapt.

Run at conversational pace: Your running pace should feel comfortably hard but not breathless. If you can't say a sentence without gasping, slow down. This is not a race — it's a training run.

Listen to your body: Mild muscle soreness and fatigue are normal and expected. Sharp pain, joint pain, or unusual fatigue are signals to rest. Pushing through pain increases injury risk.

7-Week Training Schedule

Week 1
- Run 1: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 1 min, Walk 2 min × 7 → Cool-down walk 5 min
- Run 2: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 1 min, Walk 2 min × 7 → Cool-down walk 5 min
- Run 3: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 1 min, Walk 2 min × 7 → Cool-down walk 5 min

Week 2
- All runs: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 2 min, Walk 2 min × 6 → Cool-down walk 5 min

Week 3
- All runs: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 3 min, Walk 2 min × 5 → Cool-down walk 5 min

Week 4
- All runs: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 5 min, Walk 2 min × 4 → Cool-down walk 5 min

Week 5
- All runs: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 8 min, Walk 2 min × 3 → Cool-down walk 5 min

Week 6
- All runs: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 12 min, Walk 2 min × 2 → Run 12 min → Cool-down walk 5 min

Week 7
- Run 1: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 15 min, Walk 1 min, Run 15 min → Cool-down walk 5 min
- Run 2: Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 20 min → Cool-down walk 5 min
- Run 3 (Race day or final run): Warm-up walk 5 min → Run 30 min (5 km) → Cool-down walk 5 min

Gear and Preparation

Running shoes: The most important piece of equipment. Look for shoes designed for running, with adequate cushioning and a proper fit. You should have about a thumb's width of space between your longest toe and the end of the shoe.

Clothing: Moisture-wicking fabrics reduce chafing and keep you comfortable. Avoid cotton which retains sweat and causes chafing on longer runs.

Socks: Running-specific socks with extra cushioning at heel and toe reduce blister risk.

Hydration: For runs under 30 minutes in moderate conditions, you may not need to carry water. For longer runs or hot weather, carry a water bottle or plan a route past water fountains.

Safety: In low-light conditions, wear reflective gear or carry a light. Avoid listening to music at high volume — being aware of your surroundings is important for safety.

Stretching and Flexibility

Warm up with a 5-minute brisk walk before each run — never stretch cold muscles.

After each run, spend 5–10 minutes stretching the key running muscles:
- Calf stretch: press heel to the floor with one foot back
- Quadriceps stretch: stand on one leg, pull other foot to your buttocks
- Hamstring stretch: sit and reach toward your toes
- Hip flexor stretch: lunge forward and let hips drop
- IT band stretch: cross one foot behind the other and lean to that side

Regular post-run stretching improves flexibility and reduces injury risk over time.

When You Finish a Run

Cool down with a 5-minute walk to let your heart rate and breathing return to normal before stopping.

After your workout:
- Rehydrate with water
- Eat a small protein-containing snack within 30–60 minutes if you're hungry (e.g., yoghurt, nuts, a small sandwich)
- Note how you felt during the run — this information helps you adjust future sessions

Continuing After Completing the Programme

After completing the 7-week programme, you'll have built a solid running foundation. To continue improving:

- Maintain 3 running days per week
- Increase your total weekly running distance by no more than 10% per week
- Consider entering a local 5K race for motivation and community
- Vary your running (easy days, slightly faster days) to continue improving fitness
- Explore longer goals such as a 10K programme once you're comfortable at 5K

Running is one of the most accessible and effective forms of cardiovascular exercise. With this programme, you've built a foundation that can support years of healthy running.
